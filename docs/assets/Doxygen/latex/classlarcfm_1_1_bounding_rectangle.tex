\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
A bounding rectangle for a (horizontal) 2-\/dimensional polygon. The bounding rectangle is the smallest rectangle that encloses a set of points. A point is contained in the rectangle if it lies within its boundary or on one of its edges. Thus, the bounding rectangle includes its boundary.

Special processing is conducted for \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} objects (including those embedded in \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_position}{Position}} objects. The special processing includes (1) altitude information is captured as a bound, but not used in the \char`\"{}contains\char`\"{} operation, and (2) proper handling when the longitude \char`\"{}wraps around\char`\"{} at -\/180/180. This second special processing means that a bounding rectangle for \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} is limited to 180 degrees (half the earth). Very erratic results will come from a bounding rectangle that has both \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} points and normal euclidean points.
