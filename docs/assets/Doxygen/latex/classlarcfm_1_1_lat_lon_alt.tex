\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Container to hold a latitude/longitude/altitude position.

Programmer\textquotesingle{}s note\+: There has been a tendency to have methods in \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} rely on functions in \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}}. But \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}} depends on \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} in a fundamental way. Although Java handles circular dependencies in a reasonable manner, they can be confusing and error-\/prone in many contexts. At best, circular references are dangerous. So to avoid the danger, should \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}} depend on \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} or the other way around? The choice has been made that \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}} will depend on \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}}. Therefore, please do not add any references to \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}} in this class. Over time we will remove any remaining \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}} references here. One important reason for the dependences being setup the way they are is that \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_lat_lon_alt}{Lat\+Lon\+Alt}} can be used with other Earth models besides the spherical, non-\/rotating model used in \mbox{\hyperlink{classlarcfm_1_1_great_circle}{Great\+Circle}}.
